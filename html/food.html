Danish Cuisines

Denmark's national food dish
Stegt flæsk
A few years ago, Danes were asked to vote for their national dish. And the winning dish, a classic pork recipe 'stegt flæsk med persillesovs', was no surprise. The crispy pork with parsley sauce and potatoes is a very old dish that has won the hearts, and tummies, of Danes for centuries. You can try the Danes' national dish in many restaurants around Denmark. Luckily, it is actually among the cheaper eats and if you become a fan, some restaurants even offer all-you-can-eat pork at affordable prices.
Ingredient
Roast pork , Potatoes , Parsley Sauce
Danish Pork Meatballs
Frikadeller
Frikadeller are flat, pan-fried meatballs of minced pork and veal with onions, eggs, milk, and breadcrumbs. They are one of the most popular and frequently made meals in Denmark, often served with boiled potatoes and gravy, or with rye bread and pickled vegetables.
Ingredient
Pork, Veal, Onions, Eggs, Milk, Breadcrumbs
Danish Minced Beef with Fried Onions
Hakkebøf
Danish minced beef with caramelized onions and brown sauce makes for a perfectly cozy evening meal. The dish has been around for generations, back when cattle farms supplied the freshest and most tender meats and the beef had to be minced manually.It continues to be one of the most appreciated dishes among Danish people of all ages.
Ingredient
Beef , Potatoes , Onion , Pickled gherkins / Pickled beets

Danish Dessert

Old-fashioned Danish Apple Trifle

ÆBLEKAGE

Æblekage is an old fashioned Danish apple trifle, formed of layers of soft vanilla-infused apples, sweet crunchy crumbs and some whipped cream on top. It's a simple and deliciously comforting combination, that makes a tasty, easy dessert.

The quick Danish food

Røde Pølser

Hot dogs are the street food of choice in Copenhagen. They are served with a variety of condiments, but this particular combination is a true classic...a creamy curried remoulade sauce, fresh pickled cucumber and crispy fried onoion. It might sound a bit odd, but all of these condiments are quite common in Danish cuisine and are absolutely delicious together.

Dream cake

Drømmekage

Drømmekage (lit. Dream cake) is a traditional Danish cake originating from Hjallerup in Jutland. The cake is made with a combination of flour, sugar, butter, eggs, milk, baking powder, and vanilla.

Traditional Danish Layer Cake

Lagkage

Danish lagkage, a festive layered cake, reigns supreme at Danish celebrations. Fluffy sponge layers are interwoven with creamy delights like pastry cream and fresh fruit, or tangy fruit preserves. The cake itself can be made with different batters, and the whole masterpiece is crowned with a rich blanket of whipped cream and even more fruit. From birthdays to grand occasions, lagkage is the sweet centerpiece that brings Danes together.
